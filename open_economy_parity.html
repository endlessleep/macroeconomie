Interest Rate Parities

Academic Summary — Theory, Derivations & Empirical Realities

Foundations of Interest Rate Parity

Interest rate parity links domestic interest rates, foreign interest rates, and the exchange rate market. It is the cornerstone of international finance equilibrium.

Two Core Assumptions:

  • High Capital Mobility: Capital can move across borders sufficiently and quickly to exploit return differentials.
  • Asset Substitutability: Domestic and foreign assets are close substitutes in investors' portfolios.

Uncovered Interest Rate Parity (UIP)

UIP compares returns on domestic and foreign assets when the investor does not hedge exchange rate risk. It relies on arbitrage to equalize expected returns.

Exact Equilibrium Condition
$(1 + i_{CZK,t}) = \frac{E_t(e_{CZK/EUR,t+1})}{e_{CZK/EUR,t}} (1 + i_{EUR,t})$

Where $e_{CZK/EUR}$ is the price of 1 EUR in CZK ($e \uparrow = $ depreciation of CZK).

Linear Approximation (Standard form)
$i_{CZK,t} - i_{EUR,t} \approx \frac{E_t(e_{t+1}) - e_t}{e_t}$

Interpretation: The interest rate differential between two countries should equal the expected percentage change in the exchange rate.

Why does UIP often fail in practice?

Empirically, UIP performs poorly in the short run due to several factors:

  • Exchange Rate Risk Premia: Investors demand extra compensation for the risk of holding a foreign currency.
  • Imperfect Substitutability: Not all assets are identical; local regulations and liquidity matter.
  • Expectation Errors: Market expectations are often biased or change rapidly due to news.

Covered Interest Rate Parity (CIRP)

CIRP assumes the investor hedges exchange rate risk by signing a forward contract today. This removes all uncertainty from the future conversion.

No-Arbitrage Condition (Exact)
$(1 + i_{CZK,t}) = \frac{F_{CZK/EUR,t}}{S_{CZK/EUR,t}} (1 + i_{EUR,t})$

$S_t$: Current spot exchange rate. $F_t$: Forward exchange rate agreed today.

Linear Approximation
$i_{CZK,t} - i_{EUR,t} \approx \frac{F_t - S_t}{S_t}$

Interpretation: The interest differential must equal the forward premium or discount. If not, a riskless arbitrage opportunity exists.

Reliability

In normal times, CIRP holds much better than UIP because $F_t$ is an observable market price, not an unobserved expectation.

Deviations

Small gaps can appear due to transaction costs, bid-ask spreads, or taxes. Large gaps occur during financial stress when arbitrage becomes capital-constrained.

Real Interest Parity

If we account for expected inflation, we can compare real returns across countries.

$i_{CZK,t} - E_t\pi_{CZK,t+1} = i_{EUR,t} - E_t\pi_{EUR,t+1}$

Crucial Insight: Expected real interest rates should be equal across countries. However, this is a stronger condition than UIP because it requires both capital mobility and a version of Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) in expectations.

UIP vs. CIRP: The Core Difference

Data Source UIP uses unobserved expectations; CIRP uses observed forward contracts.
Risk UIP involves exchange rate risk; CIRP is risk-free (covered).
Empirical Fit UIP fails frequently; CIRP generally holds except during systemic financial crises.
